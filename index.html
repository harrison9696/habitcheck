About

The idea behind HabitCheck is to help aid people who want to replace their 'bad habits' with 'good habits'. All it takes to benefit from this app is some self-awareness to identify unhelpful patterns carried out in everyday life and some self-reflection to pinpoint the areas in which you want to improve in. Of course, consistency is also key.

The Sciene Behind Habits

Chunking is the brains process in which it converts a sequence of actions into an automatic routine. This is at the root of how habits form.

The brain is constantly looking for ways to save effort. This effort-saving instinct is a huge advantage for two reasons. For one, an efficient brain requires less room, which makes for a small head, thus making childbirth easier. Secondly, an efficient brain allows us to 'automate' certain behaviours, requiring less mental effort which can be spent elsewhere.

The three step loop of habit formation involves a cue, a routine and a reward. A cue tells your brain to go into automatic mode and which habit to carry out. The routine, which can be physical or mental, is then carried out. Finally, the reward tells the brain if the particular loop is worth remembering for the future. Over time, as this loop repeats itself, a habit is formed.

A habit cannot be eradicated - it must be replaced. HabitCheck is built to help this process.

Rules

Everyday a 'good habit' and a 'bad habit' will be randomly assigned to you on the homepage. If you carry out the 'good habit' and avoid carrying out the 'bad habit', check their respective checkboxes and a point will be added to your score and you will gain a streak point. If you don't, your streak will be reset.

You can add and remove habits in the habits section. It's advisable to keep the number of habits low as repeatedly doing/not doing habits is beneficial to creating/breaking the habit loop.
